Here's a time line of Chadwick Boseman's life:

  • November 29, 1976 - Chadwick Aaron Boseman was born in Anderson, South Carolina, United States.
  • Early Life - Boseman grew up in Anderson, where he attended T. L. Hanna High School. He showed an interest in performing arts from a young age and wrote his first play in high school.
  • College Education - Boseman studied at Howard University in Washington, D.C., where he graduated with a Bachelor of Fine Arts degree in directing. He also attended the British American Drama Academy in Oxford, England.
  • Acting Career Beginnings - Boseman's early acting career included roles in theater productions and television shows, such as "Third Watch", "Law & Order", "ER" and in 2008 he Made his feature film debut in "The Express," playing Floyd Little.
  • Breakthrough Role - In 2013, Boseman gained widespread recognition for his portrayal of Jackie Robinson in the biographical film "42."
  • James Brown Biopic - In 2014, Boseman starred as James Brown in the biographical film "Get on Up."
  • Marvel's Black Panther - In 2016, Boseman portrayed T'Challa/Black Panther in the Marvel film "Captain America: Civil War."
  • Standalone Black Panther Film - In 2018, Boseman reprised his role as T'Challa in the standalone film "Black Panther."
  • Continued MCU Appearances - Boseman's character, Black Panther, appeared in other Marvel films, including "Avengers: Infinity War" (2018) and "Avengers: Endgame" (2019).
  • Other Major Films:
    • 2014: Appeared in the action thriller "Draft Day" as Vontae Mack.
    • 2016: Starred as Thoth, a deity from Egyptian mythology, in the fantasy film "Gods of Egypt"and as Jacob King in the action thriller "Message from the King".
    • 2017: Starred as Thurgood Marshall, the first African-American Supreme Court Justice, in the biographical film "Marshall".
    • 2019: Portrayed an NYPD detective in the crime thriller "21 Bridges", which he also produced.
    • 2020: Starred in the war drama film "Da 5 Bloods," directed by Spike Lee. He played the role of "Stormin' Norman" Earl Holloway, a fallen soldier.
  • Battle with Cancer - Boseman privately battled colon cancer, diagnosed with stage III colon cancer in 2016, which eventually progressed to stage IV.
  • Passing - Chadwick Boseman passed away on August 28, 2020, at the age of 43 in Los Angeles, California.
  • Posthumous Releases - Chadwick Boseman's final film performances, including "Ma Rainey's Black Bottom" (2020), were released after his passing.
